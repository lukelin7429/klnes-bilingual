Taiwan, in Five Parts 五個部分的台灣

Class 5A held their second online cultural exchange with ASN Senior Secondary School in India. We split the class into five groups, each one introducing a different slice of Taiwan: our snacks, our toys, our cultural objects, our currency, and our festivals.

五年甲班與印度 ASN Senior Secondary School 進行第二次線上文化交流。我們把全班分成五組，分別介紹台灣的零食、玩具、文物、貨幣與節慶。

A Dance We Will Remember 難忘的一支舞

In return, the Indian students shared their five stones game, the famous blue pottery of Jaipur, and street foods like aloo chaat and kachori.

印度同學則分享了他們的五顆石頭遊戲、著名的齋浦爾藍陶，以及 aloo chaat、kachori 等街頭小吃。

The most memorable moment came when an Indian girl, dressed in traditional clothing, performed a graceful dance — a beautiful glimpse of her country's culture. The exchange deepened our understanding of India and gave us the chance to share Taiwan with friends far away.

最難忘的一刻，是一位身穿傳統服飾的印度小女孩，優雅地表演了一支舞，展現濃厚的民族風情。這次交流加深了我們對印度的認識，也讓我們有機會向遠方的朋友介紹台灣。

“An Indian girl in traditional dress danced — and we could not look away.”

一位身著傳統服飾的印度女孩跳起舞，我們看得目不轉睛。
